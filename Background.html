Significance of the project

To the Institution:
For our project, as we assist the SAKADAB community in solving their financial issues,
we hope to impact their lives and overall lifestyle greatly. With this, we hope
to help the community stand on their own, with little, to none outside assistance.
As they start businesses and improve their agriculture, they will be able to become
much more independent and stable. This as well as community interactions will be
strengthened and will keep the members supportive of one another.

To other Students:
The significance of this project to the Ateneo students is that we will be able to live
out the many values taught to us during our stay in Ateneo. We get to practice these
as we help solve the problem that our partner institution is facing. Some examples
of the values are men and women for others, cura personalis and magis.
We have also learned a lot with the making of this project proposal.


To the Public:
This project is significant to the public because it showcases good morals of not only
students but Ateneo students. It also restores hope that the generations to come
are inputting love and care into the public, especially to those who need
it most. Not only this but it sets an example for others to step up and also spread good.

Problem identification

The problem that was identified within the community is the financial problems they
are currently facing which makes the community have problems in supplying the
needs of the people within the community. The profit they earn from the jobs
and donations to the community wasn't a reliable source of profit since most
of the profit they earn for the community are from donations.

Proposed Intervention of the Project

The proposed intervention of the project is for the community members to raise a
baking fundraiser with the people who have talent in baking and cooking in
the community. That way, the people in the SAKADAB community will be able
to supply the needs of the people in the community in a long-run within the
profit of the community they earn from the baking fundraiser.
